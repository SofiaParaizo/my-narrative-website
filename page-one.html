The Roller Coaster Journey of Artificial Intelligence: From Dreams to Reality

Artificial Intelligence (AI) has fascinated humanity for centuries, from ancient myths to futuristic visions of sentient robots. The concept of machines emulating human intelligence gained significant traction in the mid-20th century. Alan Turing, a brilliant British polymath, pondered the possibility of machines solving problems and making decisions, laying the theoretical foundation for AI.

However, the road to AI was far from smooth. In the 1950s, computers lacked the ability to store commands, hindering progress. A breakthrough occurred in 1956 when the Logic Theorist, developed by Allen Newell, Cliff Shaw, and Herbert Simon, showcased the potential of AI at the Dartmouth Summer Research Project on Artificial Intelligence (DSRPAI). This event, although not without challenges, ignited two decades of AI research.
